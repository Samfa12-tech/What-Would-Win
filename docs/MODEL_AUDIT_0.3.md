# What Would Win — model 0.3 audit

**Audit date:** 18 July 2026<br>
**Reproducibility identity:** model 0.3.0 · data 0.3.0 · share format v3<br>
**Scope:** scaling, environment, effect delivery, group aggregation, uncertainty, derived metrics, explanation output and regression coverage

> **Data follow-up:** The engine remains model 0.3.0. The later data 0.3.1 controlled-vocabulary audit, four ranged-capability corrections, exact calibration comparison and compatibility decision are recorded in `docs/SEMANTIC_DATA_AUDIT_0.3.1.md`.

## Executive conclusion

Model 0.3 corrects the largest general failure modes found in the 0.2 engine without changing the locked product direction. The numerical winner still comes from deterministic log power plus seeded Monte Carlo variation. The app still supports one profile versus effectively unlimited copies of another, selectable scaling assumptions, static hosting and abstract non-graphic reporting.

The largest changes are resolved geometry before environment evaluation, bilateral stopping and attack access, explicit active frontage with logarithmic reserve weighting, bounded-arena occupancy caps, bounded area control, continuous ranged-resource effects, removal of the conceptual confidence jump and a reconstructable factor ledger that drives a structured encounter explanation.

This remains an entertainment model. The audit improves internal consistency and exposes more of the calculation; it does not turn hypothetical combat into an empirical prediction.

## Audit method

The review traced every deterministic adjustment from profile resolution to result output, then challenged the engine with:

- extreme enlargement and reduction under all three scaling modes;
- quantity-one role reversal;
- equal-total-biomass fragmentation;
- tiny attackers against very large defenders;
- flight and medium-access mismatches at ordinary and conceptual quantities;
- resized profiles in water and constrained terrain;
- prepared, ranged, escape and resource-sensitive scenarios;
- the quantity boundary where results become conceptual;
- narrative claims against the factors actually recorded by the engine.

The 16 calibration fixtures remain behavioural guardrails, not declarations of objective truth. Focused invariant tests supplement them so a popular matchup cannot be improved by silently breaking unrelated archetypes.

## Findings and model 0.3 response

### 1. Small-profile mass offset

**Finding:** the former `log10(M + 0.02)` term gave a disproportionate power gift to profiles below roughly 20 g and distorted extreme reduction.

**Response:** model 0.3 uses `log10(max(M, 10^-6))`. The positive floor protects numerical stability without adding meaningful body mass.

### 2. Baseline geometry surviving resizing

**Finding:** some environment and water checks used baseline mass or height after a profile had been resized. A giant small animal could retain small-animal terrain treatment, and immersion could be evaluated against the wrong height.

**Response:** target mass, linear scale, body length, body height, reach and speed are resolved before environment, water, frontage and occupancy calculations. Data 0.3 also distinguishes explicit `amphibious` and `land-capable` traits from the broad aquatic capability flag. Positive water depth can flood nominally land terrain; medium access is derived from depth relative to each resolved body height rather than the terrain label alone.

### 3. Asymmetric penetration logic

**Finding:** the group paid an explicit penetration penalty while the solo side did not face the same effect-delivery test. Low surface armour could also erase a large body-depth and momentum mismatch for tiny attackers.

**Response:** both sides now receive the same stopping calculation, separated into protection and mass barriers with bounded delivery-specific bypasses. A successful touch is not automatically treated as decisive stopping power.

### 4. Binary or incomplete access handling

**Finding:** flight, range and medium mismatch could produce broad bonuses or penalties without clearly limiting how many attackers could exploit an opening. Large quantities could eventually overpower an opponent they could not credibly reach.

**Response:** bilateral attack access now considers flight, medium, distance, usable range and remaining resources. Ranged contribution changes continuously with access and resources. Severe group-access mismatch imposes a smooth effective-pressure ceiling, so more reserves do not create new lines of attack by themselves.

### 5. Quantity exponent standing in for contact geometry

**Finding:** model 0.2 described contact saturation but primarily applied `E × log10(N)`. It did not expose how many bodies could engage or how reserves contributed.

**Response:** model 0.3 estimates active frontage from resized defender span, attacker width, terrain and engagement mode. Quantity beyond the front contributes through a bounded logarithmic reserve weight. Aggregation uses effective log quantity rather than the declared quantity directly. In a bounded arena, usable quantity is capped at an approximate single-layer occupancy before frontage, reserve and access limits are applied; the declared quantity is retained and the mismatch is reported. Open arenas remain available for explicitly aggregate scenarios.

### 6. Unbounded or duplicated scale leverage

**Finding:** naive fixes could count the mass advantage in core power, stopping and area control strongly enough to overcorrect the original problem. Fragmenting a fixed biomass could also create too much aggregate power.

**Response:** the supplemental mass-stopping coefficient is deliberately moderated because the core mass term already carries most scale leverage. Solo area control is bounded against effective pressure. Fixed-biomass and cross-scaling regression tests constrain, rather than claim to eliminate, fragmentation artefacts.

### 7. Conceptual confidence discontinuity

**Finding:** crossing the conceptual threshold changed epistemic compression to `0.99`, making a physically impossible scenario appear more certain solely because its quantity crossed a display boundary.

**Response:** confidence-based compression is continuous across the threshold. Conceptual results widen the model-sensitivity floor and add a strong applicability warning instead of increasing certainty.

### 8. Literal-looking derived metrics

**Finding:** duration did not fully reflect selected starting distance, and duration/loss text remained present at quantities that cannot be physically staged.

**Response:** ordinary duration uses effective separation—capped to arena diameter when bounded—and effective pressure. Escape can reduce expected losses, bounded-arena loss counts use only the arena-usable force, and wording follows the selected win condition. Conceptual duration and loss counts are deliberately withheld.

### 9. Generic battle prose

**Finding:** the former short narrative could sound plausible while failing to show which adjustments actually produced the result. It risked being read as a simulated sequence of individual actions.

**Response:** every material deterministic adjustment is recorded in an applied-factor ledger. Ordinary reports use seven factor-linked phases; conceptual reports use three aggregate phases. The result includes an alternate path and caveats, but remains an explanation of the calculation—not a sampled event trace, anatomy simulation or independent source of the winner.

## Focused regression coverage

Model 0.3 adds or strengthens checks that:

- the audited dog-sized-mouse scenario keeps stopping, frontage and reserve effects visible;
- swapping solo/group roles at quantity one reverses the deterministic margin without inventing a group bonus;
- fragmenting fixed total biomass cannot produce an unconstrained power gain;
- severe access mismatch remains capped even at `10^100` opponents;
- resized height, rather than baseline height, controls water immersion;
- crossing the conceptual threshold does not jump toward certainty;
- every narrative factor reference resolves to the applied ledger;
- conceptual reports avoid literal duration, casualty and staging claims;
- effective starting distance affects duration and bounded declarations are reported at the same capped separation used by the mechanics;
- preparation benefits remain capped;
- open-arena escape reduces expected losses;
- real-profile rhinoceros/mouse, eagle/mouse and orca/wolf edge cases remain calibrated.

Browser coverage verifies the user-visible phase sequence, heuristic labels, conceptual withholding, applied-factor ledger and old-model migration warning in the production build. Final command counts belong in `docs/QA_REPORT.md`.

## Remaining risks and recommended next work

1. **Archetype specificity:** replace global frontage, reserve, strict-scaling and stopping coefficients with separately reviewed terrestrial, flying, aquatic, swarm, pack and formation functions.
2. **Sensitivity:** add global sensitivity analysis and show which uncertain inputs can reverse a verdict.
3. **Provenance:** expand expert-reviewed per-field sources and uncertainty ranges beyond the initial high-use profiles.
4. **Arena feasibility:** replace the coarse single-layer occupancy estimate with reviewed deployment, reinforcement and multi-level geometry if greater spatial fidelity is justified.
5. **Time and injury:** research phased incapacitation, delayed venom and resource exhaustion without introducing graphic output or false anatomical precision.
6. **Physical QA:** run manual assistive-technology, physical Safari/iOS and deployment-subpath tests before public hosting.

Do not replace these improvements with an AI-generated winner. A future assistant may help users create structured inputs or restyle already-calculated explanations, but versioned numerical output must remain authoritative and reproducible.
